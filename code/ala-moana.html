Poem

An elderly clarinetist
Flits a solo off his fingers
On the edge of the pier
By the not-yet-sunken yachts.

The not-quite-as-elderly pair up
And start swirling
To the music.

They glide gracefully,
Sashay tastefully,
Reminisce forgetfully,
Slide twinklingly
Off into the frisky moonlight.
